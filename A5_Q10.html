Q10. Create a web page to display definition list which defines the terms: B2B, B2C, C2B, C2C.

B2B
B2B e-commerce, short for business-to-business electronic commerce, is the sale of goods or services between businesses via an online sales portal. In general, it is used to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of a companyâ€™s sales efforts.
Examples: Amazon, Indiamart, TradeIndia

B2C
B2C (Business-to-Customer) ecommerce is the exchange of goods or services over the internet between online stores and individual customers.
Examples: Myntra, Flipkart, Alibaba

C2B
Consumer-to-business (C2B) is a business model in which consumers (individuals) create value and businesses consume that value.
Examples: Timesjobs, Upwork, Freelancer

C2C
Customer to customer (C2C) is a business model whereby customers can trade with each other, typically in an online environment.
Two implementations of C2C markets are auctions and classified advertisements.
Examples: OLX, Olacabs, Quikr, Zomato
